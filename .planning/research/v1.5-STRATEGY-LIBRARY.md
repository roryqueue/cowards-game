# v1.5 Research: Advanced Strategy Library and Tournament Evidence

**Project:** Coward's Game
**Milestone:** v1.5 Strategy Workshop Power Tools and Advanced Strategy Library
**Researched:** 2026-05-20
**Mode:** Ecosystem and product validation research
**Overall confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH

## Findings

### Existing Coward's Game evidence

- v1.4 shipped a credible evidence base, not just fixtures: `/ladder/v1-4-demo` has 8 starter-derived entrants, 2 counted MatchSets, 96 replay-backed Chronicles, `chronicle-v1.4`, `cowards-rules-v1.4`, and browser-verified ladder, MatchSet, replay, Strategy card, and player pages.
- The v1.4 demo result is intentionally useful but not diverse enough to become the v1.5 seed set by itself. Backstab Hunter finished 23-1-0, followed by Aggro Chaser and Wall Press. Those are good benchmark opponents, but copying their shape would overfit v1.5 toward rear-arc pressure, direct chase, and wall pressure.
- v1.4 metric signals are rich enough to validate archetype behavior: 25,391 Cycle starts, 3,292 skipped Activations, 19,556 Advances, 303 blocked moves, 516 Backstab resolutions, and 114 contractions. v1.5 should promote these from script output into reusable library/tournament evidence summaries.
- Current starter metadata already supports the right public vocabulary: stable starter ID, version, description, tags, doctrine notes, expected behavior, uses-memory flag, source hash, source bytes, validation state, and lineage. v1.5 should extend this model instead of replacing it.
- Current public surfaces already enforce the privacy shape v1.5 needs: public result DTOs, Strategy cards, player pages, and replay projections must not expose Strategy source, StrategyMemory, SoldierMemory, objective payloads, raw owner debug, or private runtime internals.

### External product patterns that apply

- Competitive strategy products present "tier" or "viability" as a metagame explanation, not a pure leaderboard. Smogon viability rankings explicitly rank usable options across offensive, defensive, and support roles in one metagame and acknowledge that roles and rankings change over time. For Coward's Game, this argues for archetype-role cards and evidence bands rather than a single "best Strategy" list.
- Metagame reporting should separate popularity/representation from performance. Magic: The Gathering metagame reports publish archetype share and non-mirror win rates, link each archetype to a representative real list, and discuss why a previously dominant archetype may become poorly positioned. For v1.5, the analogous public page should show each Advanced Strategy's role, field share in curated examples, aggregate record, non-mirror matchup record, representative MatchSets, and caveats.
- Matchup data needs sample size and uncertainty labels. Current MTG methodology pages commonly include match counts and confidence/uncertainty signals behind win rates. Coward's Game can use simpler labels for v1.5: `thin evidence`, `supported`, and `strong evidence`, based on completed replay-backed Match counts per matchup.
- Bot-programming ecosystems emphasize replay review over rank alone. CodinGame guidance says rankings do not explain what went wrong, but replays do; Battlecode emphasizes autonomous bots refined through scrimmages and tournaments under a written rules specification. Coward's Game should therefore publish replay handoff links and behavior-callout summaries for every library/tournament claim.
- Small tournament demos are best served by round-robin or group round-robin evidence before bracket drama. Challonge documents round robin as every competitor playing every other competitor, Swiss as non-elimination equal-play grouping, and two-stage tournaments as pool/regular-season play feeding finals. For v1.5's 8-10 entrants, a complete round-robin or two groups plus finals is more informative than single elimination.
- Public leaderboard overfitting is a real failure mode. The Ladder paper frames adaptive repeated evaluation against a visible leaderboard as an overfitting risk. v1.5 is not building durable ratings, but the same product lesson applies: do not tune advanced seed Strategies solely against the visible v1.4 winner order or one public tournament table.

### Product direction

v1.5 should treat the Advanced Strategy Library as a "teaching metagame" and benchmark suite. The purpose is to show credible doctrine diversity and give authors hard opponents to test against, not to declare a permanent official tier list.

Recommended framing:

- **Starter Library:** readable, beginner-forkable Strategies that teach basic mechanics.
- **Advanced Library:** stronger seed/benchmark Strategies that demonstrate specific tactical ideas and expose common weaknesses.
- **Example MatchSets:** curated evidence packets that show why an archetype exists.
- **Demo Tournament:** a completed, replay-backed v1.5 advanced-only tournament that proves the seed set behaves realistically without becoming an official public tournament promise.

Recommended Advanced Strategy roster target:

| Slot | Primary archetype | Suggested doctrine | Must prove |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Pressure/contact | Contact Compressor | Forces adjacency/contact without collapsing into reckless chase. |
| 2 | Anti-backstab positioning | Rear-Guard Pivot | Reduces rear-arc exposure against Backstab Hunter-like pressure. |
| 3 | Wall control | Wall Anvil | Uses edges to constrain enemy movement while avoiding self-trapping. |
| 4 | Center control | Center Lock | Holds shrinking-board tempo without becoming passive turtle play. |
| 5 | Contraction survival | Shrink Prophet | Survives into contraction and converts shrink pressure into advantage. |
| 6 | Evasive mobility | Vector Escape | Escapes pins and preserves mobility under interleaved Cycle pressure. |
| 7 | Trap/control | Baited Gate | Creates punish windows against linear contact Strategies. |
| 8 | Mirror-breaking/adaptive | Phase Switch | Breaks symmetric or repeated-state play with readable mode changes. |
| 9 | Late-cycle stabilization | Cycle Banker | Stabilizes after blocked moves, skipped Activations, or late-cycle danger. |
| 10 | Memory-based response | Grudge Ledger | Uses SoldierMemory/StrategyMemory to respond differently to observed opponent pressure. |

Eight or nine Strategies are acceptable only if omitted slots are explicitly deferred and the remaining set has stronger evidence. If quality is equal, prefer ten because the milestone goal is to create a broad benchmark field.

### Evidence model

Every Advanced Strategy should ship with a compact evidence packet:

- Metadata: advanced ID, version `v1.5`, primary archetype, secondary tags, difficulty, uses-memory flag, rule compatibility, runtime compatibility, source hash, source bytes, validation state, and safe lineage.
- Doctrine summary: three to five public notes explaining intent without exposing private implementation details beyond what the Strategy author already publishes in the library.
- Public performance: W-L-D, points, non-mirror record, top favorable matchups, top weak matchups, contraction survival count, Backstab involvement, movement/blocking signal, STONE/FALLEN outcomes, and representative replay links.
- Evidence links: one or more starter gauntlet MatchSets, advanced-vs-advanced MatchSets, example MatchSets, and tournament MatchSets.
- Caveats: sample size label, rule version, generated-at time, runtime adapter metadata, and "not a durable rating" language.

The strongest public presentation is an archetype matrix, not just standings:

| Strategy | Archetype | Aggregate | Favored into | Weak into | Behavior evidence | Sample |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rear-Guard Pivot | Anti-backstab | 14-10-2 | Backstab pressure | Center lock | Low rear-arc losses, high turn-away recovery | Supported |

This matrix should be generated from replay-backed DTOs or explicit validation summaries, never hand-entered as marketing copy.

### Validation shape

v1.5 should use three layers of deterministic validation:

1. **Authoring validation:** each Strategy source validates, respects source/memory/runtime limits, produces legal SoldierBrain output, and has no system failures in smoke execution.
2. **Behavior validation:** each Strategy demonstrates its claimed archetype through Chronicle-derived signals in at least one curated MatchSet. Example: a contraction survival Strategy must have replay-backed contraction survival, not merely a tag.
3. **Metagame validation:** the set as a whole has matchup diversity. No single Strategy should dominate every archetype, and no included Strategy should be a helpless filler entrant.

Recommended gauntlets:

- **Starter gauntlet:** every Advanced Strategy vs all 10 v1.4 Starter Strategies. Purpose: prove the advanced set is stronger/more nuanced than the teaching baseline.
- **Advanced round robin:** all Advanced Strategies against each other, preferably with top/bottom orientation covered by existing MatchSet generation.
- **Counter gauntlets:** curated MatchSets for archetype claims, such as Anti-Backstab vs Backstab Hunter/Contact Compressor, Trap Control vs Aggro Chaser/Contact Compressor, Contraction Survival vs Center Lock/Wall Anvil.
- **Regression gauntlet:** the v1.4 top three, Backstab Hunter, Aggro Chaser, and Wall Press, remain fixed benchmark opponents. They should inform tuning but not become the only optimization target.

Recommended review triggers:

- A Strategy exceeds roughly 75% match win rate against the entire advanced field and has no losing or close matchup.
- A Strategy loses roughly 85%+ of non-mirror matches and has no favorable/close matchup that demonstrates its role.
- Three or more Advanced Strategies share the same primary tactical pattern or are obvious descendants of the v1.4 top winners.
- A claimed archetype lacks Chronicle-derived evidence for its behavior.
- The tournament champion wins mainly through runtime failures, invalid outputs, non-counted MatchSets, or obvious exploit behavior rather than legal tactical play.
- Public output contains source, StrategyMemory, SoldierMemory, objective payloads, owner debug, raw Awareness Grid internals, or private runtime data.

These are review triggers, not automatic balance failures. The project should preserve specialists, counters, and non-transitive relationships even when their aggregate records are uneven.

## Recommended v1.5 Requirements

### Must-have requirements

1. **Advanced Library Tier:** The app defines a distinct Advanced Strategy Library tier separate from the Starter Library, with target 10 Strategies and minimum 8 only when quality/diversity evidence is documented.
2. **Archetype Coverage:** Each Advanced Strategy has exactly one primary archetype and optional secondary tags. The set must cover pressure/contact, anti-backstab positioning, wall control, center control, contraction survival, evasive mobility, trap/control, mirror-breaking/adaptive play, late-cycle stabilization, and memory-based opponent response, or explicitly justify any missing archetype.
3. **Immutable v1.5 Lineage:** Advanced Strategy Revisions are generated as immutable system-owned seed Revisions with `cowards-rules-v1.4` compatibility unless a newer rule version exists, `starter/advanced` lineage metadata, source hash, validation status, and generation provenance.
4. **Public Strategy Cards:** Public Advanced Strategy cards show archetype, doctrine notes, tags, validation state, source hash, source size, rule/runtime compatibility, safe lineage, evidence links, and public records without exposing Strategy source, StrategyMemory, SoldierMemory, objective payloads, owner debug, or private runtime internals by default.
5. **Workshop Fork/Apply Path:** Users can intentionally fork or apply Advanced Strategies from the Workshop in the same controlled style as Starter Strategies, with clear labeling that these are stronger seed opponents/templates.
6. **Starter Gauntlet Evidence:** Every Advanced Strategy is run against all 10 v1.4 Starters, and the generated evidence records W-L-D, counted MatchSets, replay links, rule version, Chronicle version, runtime adapter, and privacy-safe public DTO checks.
7. **Advanced Round Robin Evidence:** The Advanced set is run against itself in a deterministic advanced-only MatchSet suite with replay-backed public results and a generated matchup matrix.
8. **Behavior Signal Extraction:** Validation extracts Chronicle-derived signals relevant to archetype claims: contraction participation, Backstab resolutions, Advances, blocked movement recovery, STONE/FALLEN outcomes, skipped Activations, match terminal reasons, and representative replay links.
9. **Example MatchSets:** v1.5 publishes curated example MatchSets for at least five archetype interactions, including anti-backstab, wall/edge control, center control vs mobility, contraction survival, and trap/control vs pressure.
10. **Completed Demo Tournament:** v1.5 generates one completed realistic advanced-only demo tournament with at least 8 entrants, counted MatchSets only, standings/bracket or stage summary, MatchSet result links, replay links, provenance, and a human-readable validation summary.
11. **Non-Durable Framing:** Library/tournament copy must state that v1.5 evidence is demo/benchmark evidence, not durable all-time ratings, official public tournament operations, or permanent balance truth.
12. **Dominance Review Gate:** The v1.5 generation/validation script must flag degenerate winner-take-all outcomes using review triggers such as no losing matchups for the champion, helpless filler entrants, too many derivatives of v1.4 winners, or missing archetype behavior evidence.
13. **Privacy Regression Tests:** Public Strategy, MatchSet, tournament, ladder, player, and replay DTOs/pages used by v1.5 evidence must retain leak tests for Strategy source, StrategyMemory, SoldierMemory, objective payloads, owner debug, raw Awareness Grid details, and private runtime internals.
14. **Browser Verification:** The milestone verifies the Advanced Library page, representative Strategy cards, example MatchSet result pages, completed tournament page, player/profile pages if used, and representative replays in browser.

### Nice-to-have requirements

1. **Evidence Bands:** Label each Strategy/matchup as `thin evidence`, `supported`, or `strong evidence` based on completed replay-backed match count.
2. **Matchup Heatmap:** Add a small row-vs-column matchup matrix on the Advanced Library or tournament page.
3. **Counter Badges:** Show `pressures`, `checks`, and `struggles into` badges derived from matchup evidence.
4. **Strategy Compare Entry Point:** Link each Advanced Strategy card to Workshop comparison/diff against its nearest Starter or v1.4 benchmark.
5. **Tournament Stage View:** Present the demo tournament as group round robin plus finals if implementation cost is modest; otherwise complete round robin is enough.
6. **Validation Export:** Save a JSON summary artifact from the generation script so roadmap/audit phases can compare numbers without scraping pages.
7. **Replay Callout Anchors:** Deep-link representative replay timestamps for key behavior signals when replay routing supports it.

## Pitfalls/Risks

### Critical risks

- **Overfitting to v1.4 winners:** Backstab Hunter, Aggro Chaser, and Wall Press should become benchmarks and counter-design targets, not the template for most Advanced Strategies. Mitigation: cap same-pattern derivatives, require primary archetype coverage, and tune against starter, advanced, and curated counter gauntlets.
- **Shipping a ranked ladder in disguise:** A polished tournament page can accidentally imply official durable rankings. Mitigation: call it a completed v1.5 demo tournament or benchmark tournament, include rule/version/provenance, and avoid all-time rating language.
- **Public privacy regression:** Advanced cards and evidence pages will be tempting places to show "why it works." Mitigation: keep public explanation at doctrine/metric/replay level; source is available only through intended fork/apply or owner-authorized paths, never through public result DTOs.
- **Aggregate win-rate monoculture:** Pure win-rate optimization will discard specialists and counters. Mitigation: acceptance must require behavior evidence and matchup diversity, not only top standings.
- **Hand-authored evidence drift:** Manually written claims can become stale after regeneration. Mitigation: generate evidence tables from MatchSet/Chronicle summaries and include generated-at/provenance labels.

### Moderate risks

- **Too many memory Strategies:** Memory-based play is important, but if most advanced seeds are memory-heavy, users may learn that basic positional logic is obsolete. Mitigation: mix stateless and memory Strategies, and label difficulty honestly.
- **Weak archetype labels:** Tags like "adaptive" or "control" are not enough unless replay metrics show the behavior. Mitigation: require per-archetype proof signals before publication.
- **Unclear sample size:** Small deterministic fields can make a 70% matchup look more certain than it is. Mitigation: show match count and evidence band.
- **Tournament drama overriding educational value:** Brackets are exciting but can hide matchup texture. Mitigation: prefer full round robin or group round robin before finals.
- **Derivative source similarity:** Advanced seeds copied from Starters with tiny tweaks will not teach new doctrines. Mitigation: require explicit doctrine differences, new source hashes, and a validation summary explaining why each advanced seed earns its slot.

### Minor risks

- **Naming churn:** Strategy names should be vivid but still map to canonical terms and public tags.
- **Page density:** A full matrix can overwhelm. Use summary cards first and detailed tables lower on the page.
- **Dead replay links:** Generation scripts should fail if public MatchSet or replay URLs do not resolve.

## Acceptance Criteria Ideas

### Advanced Library acceptance

- The Advanced Library contains 10 v1.5 Strategies, or 8-9 with a written validation note explaining why omitted archetypes are deferred.
- No two Strategies share the same primary archetype unless all required archetypes are already covered.
- At least three Advanced Strategies are stateless and at least three use StrategyMemory or SoldierMemory, unless validation shows a stronger diversity reason.
- Every Advanced Strategy validates, executes without system failure, has a unique source hash, and has public metadata with rule/runtime compatibility.
- Public cards and API DTOs pass privacy assertions for source, memory, objectives, owner debug, raw Awareness Grid, and private runtime data.

### Evidence acceptance

- A generated starter gauntlet includes every Advanced Strategy against all 10 v1.4 Starters.
- A generated advanced round robin includes every Advanced Strategy against every other Advanced Strategy and produces counted MatchSets only.
- Each Strategy has at least one representative replay link for its claimed archetype behavior.
- Each claimed archetype has at least one Chronicle-derived metric in the validation summary.
- The validation summary includes standings, matchup matrix, behavior-signal table, review-trigger results, rule version, Chronicle version, runtime adapter, generated-at timestamp, and representative URLs.

### Diversity acceptance

- The champion has at least one close or unfavorable advanced-field matchup, or the validation summary explicitly explains why the set still avoids degeneracy.
- Every included Strategy has at least one close or favorable matchup that demonstrates its reason to exist.
- The top three v1.4 demo winners are included as fixed benchmark opponents or direct comparison rows, but no more than three Advanced Strategies are primarily tuned as direct descendants of those doctrines.
- At least five curated example MatchSets demonstrate non-transitive or counter-style relationships, not only strongest-vs-weakest stomps.
- A human reviewer can inspect the generated matrix and identify at least four distinct tactical families with different strengths and weaknesses.

### Tournament acceptance

- A completed v1.5 advanced-only demo tournament page is reachable locally and shows entrants, format, standings or stage results, MatchSet links, replay links, counted status, provenance, and non-durable demo framing.
- Tournament MatchSets are complete, counted, and replay-backed; degraded/system-failed/non-counted results do not decide standings.
- The tournament page links to public Strategy cards and player/profile pages where applicable without exposing private data.
- Browser verification covers tournament page, at least two MatchSet pages, at least two replays, at least two Advanced Strategy cards, and the Advanced Library entry point.

### Tuning acceptance

- Tuning notes name which v1.4 evidence influenced each Advanced Strategy and which non-v1.4 benchmark prevented overfitting.
- Review triggers are computed and included in the validation summary.
- Any Strategy changed after a failed review gate is regenerated with new source hash/provenance and rerun through the affected gauntlets.

## Agreement Points Needed From Workshop UX Research

- **Library placement:** Should Advanced Strategies appear in the existing Workshop Starter Library panel as a tier/filter, or as a separate Advanced Library surface with its own route?
- **Source visibility model:** In the Workshop, does selecting an Advanced Strategy immediately load readable source like Starters, or does it require an explicit "Fork/apply" action first? Public pages must remain source-private either way.
- **Comparison depth:** What is the minimum useful comparison view for v1.5: metadata/result deltas only, source diff, or source diff plus replay-linked behavior deltas?
- **Evidence vocabulary:** Confirm user-facing labels for evidence bands, dominance warnings, matchup confidence, and non-durable demo tournament framing.
- **Gauntlet UX:** Decide whether users run test matrices from a Strategy detail panel, a dedicated gauntlet tab, or an inline Workshop action.
- **Replay handoff:** Define how a result row links into replay context: whole Match replay is enough for v1.5, or deep-linked event/callout anchors are required.
- **Archetype tags:** Confirm the public tag taxonomy so library cards, Strategy pages, MatchSet evidence, and Workshop filters use the same labels.
- **Tournament presentation:** Decide whether the completed demo tournament should look like a ladder season, a tournament-specific page, or a generated MatchSet collection with a summary header.
